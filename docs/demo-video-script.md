# FairShot: tech video script

Target: 3 to 3.5 minutes. Screen recording of the app with your voice over it. Paul's read-friendly line-per-beat script. Production setup first, then scene by scene.

## Before recording

1. Production server is running (Claude handles: npm run build, npm start, fresh seed, Priya memo, Lena primed).
2. Browser: hide the bookmarks bar, close spare tabs, 100% zoom.
3. Mic check. The Load demo application button fills Amara's application for you.
4. Have your spoken answer for Amara's first question ready, and say "the e-rostering guidance NHS England publishes" somewhere in it: that is the claim the live check corroborates on camera.
5. Amara's exact scores can vary a point or two run to run (twelve and fifty-four are typical). If the screen shows a different number, say the number on the screen.
6. After Amara's first answer, pause recording and tell Claude "finish it": the interview completes, her memo generates, and a decision gets recorded so the "under an hour" line in Scene 5 is true.
7. Record at 1080p or better. OBS, Loom, or Windows Game Bar all fine.

## Scene 1: The problem (20s)

SCREEN: FairShot dashboard, freshly seeded.

Right now, venture capital runs on networks.

If a founder knows the right people, they get seen.

If they don't?

Their story stays scattered across repos and posts that nobody reads closely.

FairShot is a VC operating system built to fix that.

It finds overlooked founders...

Assesses them the way a talent team would...

And gets them an evidence-backed, hundred-thousand-dollar decision...

Inside twenty-four hours.

## Scene 2: The thesis (15s)

SCREEN: Thesis page. Point at sectors, check size, conviction threshold.

Everything starts with the investor's thesis.

Sectors, stage, geography, check size, and risk appetite.

Nothing is hardcoded here.

Change a setting...

And every single score in the system changes with it.

## Scene 3: Sourcing, live (40s)

SCREEN: Pipeline page. Click Run outbound scan. Watch new cards appear in Sourced and Screened. Type a GitHub handle into the Source founder box and run it.

Sourcing is where FairShot goes deepest.

Watch it work live.

It's scanning GitHub, Hacker News, and arXiv right now.

These are real founders and real signals—no stale datasets.

Everything lands in one funnel, gets clean deduplication, and screens against the thesis.

When someone hits that threshold?

They're invited to interview automatically.

It does targeted sourcing, too.

Give it any GitHub handle, and it builds an evidence profile from their real footprint.

Repos, followers, stars, and blogs.

It even has an identity gate to filter out people with similar names.

When it profiled me, it found my real repos...

And correctly excluded two other Pauls.

## Scene 4: The cold start, Amara (75s)

SCREEN: Apply page. Click Load demo application, submit. Point at pre-interview brief. Click I'm ready. Click voice toggle, let it read, answer with mic (mention NHS England's e-rostering guidance). Watch for "Checked while you spoke" chip. Cut to completed interview feedback card in another tab.

Here's the case that matters most:

The founder the industry cannot see.

Amara spent eleven years running hospital staffing rotas.

She built a tool that three wards use.

But she has no GitHub, no investors, and no network.

On paper, she's invisible.

Her application scores a twelve, because almost nothing can be verified from the outside.

So, FairShot interviews her.

First, it briefs her.

Here's what we'll dig into, here's what we couldn't confirm—bring proof if you have it.

Then comes the interview.

It's a Socratic, behavioral interview—the same craft a talent assessor uses.

Every question anchors to her actual evidence, probing for what she personally did.

Watch what happens while she answers:

It's fact-checking her in real-time.

She mentions the e-rostering guidance NHS England publishes...

And before the next question arrives, it finds the source, links it, and shows it on screen.

If it can't find a public record, it says that too, and asks who could confirm it.

Diligence isn't a stage that happens after the conversation. It's happening inside it.

Notice what it corroborated: the public context.

Amara's own work is exactly what can't be publicly checked...

Which is why the interviewer asks who can back it up.

When it ends, her capability is fully visible.

Her Founder Score moves from twelve to fifty-four.

That's fifty-four with medium confidence.

The memo says precisely which parts are backed by facts...

And which parts are still her word alone.

It's honest, not generous.

And she gets this report: what we saw, where evidence was thin, and how to strengthen her next application.

Funded or not, every founder gets that back.

## Scene 5: The investor side (45s)

SCREEN: Memo page for Priya Nair. Scroll slowly: three axes, capability panel, click one evidence link, gaps box, click "Watch" (or click "Invest" and use override line).

The investor sees three independent lenses, never averaged:

Founder, Market, and the Idea.

Each has its own direction of travel.

Underneath is the capability assessment.

Every single score links to the exact evidence behind it.

Click it, and you're looking at the claim and its sources.

The memo drafts itself entirely from memory.

What it can't verify, it flags.

What's missing, it highlights instead of inventing—like missing financials.

Then, the decision.

One click logs how long it took from first signal to money decision.

For Amara, that was under an hour.

Notice the memo's own verdict here is WATCH.

I can overrule it...

But if I do, the record shows the human disagreed with the evidence.

It's decision support that keeps the human honest, too.

## Scene 6: Ask it anything (15s)

SCREEN: Dashboard query bar. Type: technical founder, Europe, AI infra, no prior VC backing. Show results with match reasons.

And the entire memory is queryable in plain English.

One compound question, one pass...

And every result explains exactly why it matched.

It even understands nuance...

Like knowing that 'never raised money' is a massive point in a founder's favor.

## Scene 7: Close (20s)

SCREEN: Dashboard, scroll to the "What FairShot deliberately ignores" panel.

FairShot deliberately ignores where you went to school...

Who referred you...

Or what brand names sit on your CV.

We wrote a hard rule into the code:

Real people are prioritized and never judged until they choose to take part.

It even assessed me—the builder—and told me:

"Watch, don't invest. Show me traction."

That's the point.

A system honest enough to say that, is a system a founder can trust with their shot.

FairShot was built solo in twenty-four hours...

So that funding decisions take twenty-four hours, too.
